InsMapper: Exploring Inner-Instance Information for Vectorized HD Mapping

Zhenhua Xu, Kwan-Yee K. Wong, Hengshuang Zhao
The University of Hong Kong
Paper Code

Abstract

Vectorized high-definition (HD) maps contain detailed information about surrounding road elements, which are crucial for various downstream tasks in modern autonomous driving vehicles, such as vehicle planning and control. Recent works have attempted to directly detect the vectorized HD map as a point set prediction task, resulting in significant improvements in detection performance. However, these approaches fail to analyze and exploit the inner-instance correlations between predicted points, impeding further advancements. To address these challenges, we investigate the utilization of inner-INStance information for vectorized hIGH-definition mapping through Transformers and introduce InsMapper. This paper presents three novel designs within InsMapper that leverage inner-instance information in distinct ways, including hybrid query generation, inner-instance query fusion, and inner-instance feature aggregation. Comparative experiments are conducted on the NuScenes dataset, showcasing the superiority of our proposed method. InsMapper surpasses previous state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods by 5.78 mAP and 5.12 TOPO, which assess topology correctness. Simultaneously, InsMapper maintains high efficiency during both training and inference phases, resulting in remarkable comprehensive performance.
